Visiting the Galapagos Islands

I'm planning a trip to the Galapagos Islands. Here's some information about them, as well as why I've decided to go:

Facts About the Galapagos:

  • There are no natural predators in the Galapagos Islands.
  • There are 19 islands in the archipelago.
  • The Galapagos Islands are located 620 miles off the coast of Ecuador.
  • In the Pacific Ocean.
  • The land and waters of the Galapagos Islands are protected World Heritage sites.
  • Each island is different from the next!

Why Visit?

  • Charles Darwin discovered evolution there!
  • Beautiful nature!
  • Global warming threatens all World Heritage sites--see them while you still can!

When to visit?

  • June, July, and August are the busiest months for Galapagos tourism.
  • The rainy season is December-May, with temperatures in the 80s.
  • The climate is cooler and dryer during the rainy season in June-Novemner.
  • Visit during the dry season if you like to SCUBA dive--that's when you're most likely to see hammerhead or even whale sharks!

How to get there?

  • You can't visit without a guide. Guides must be licensed with the Galapagos National Park.
  • Take a day trip from Puerto Ayora, Santa Cruz.
  • Book a boat trip with companies like Adventure Life or National Geographic.
  • Tours book up months in advance, so make sure you book early!

Activities!

  • SCUBA diving and snorkeling.
  • Hiking.
  • Surfing.
  • Kayaking.
  • Wildlife observation!
